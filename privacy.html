Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 22, 2026

OGENTI, Inc. ("we", "us", "our") operates the ogenti desktop application and ogenti.com website (collectively, the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect personal information in compliance with the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA) of the Republic of Korea, the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other applicable data protection laws.

1. Personal Information Controller

The entity responsible for your personal information is:

  • Company: OGENTI, Inc.
  • Location: Seoul, Republic of Korea
  • Privacy Officer: Juwon Ha (CEO)
  • Contact: privacy@ogenti.com

2. Information We Collect

  • Account Data — Email address and display name when you create an account. Collected upon registration with your consent.
  • Application Data (Beta) — Name, email, reason for interest, and virtual bank account information submitted through the beta application form.
  • Transaction Data — Credit purchase/sell records, Marketplace transactions, and payment metadata processed through Stripe.
  • Usage Data — Anonymous, aggregated usage statistics such as feature usage frequency and session duration. This data cannot identify individual users.
  • Agent Data — All agent configurations, conversation logs, and execution history are stored locally on your device only. We do not have access to this data.
  • Technical Data — IP address, browser type, OS version, and device identifiers collected automatically for security and service maintenance.

We do not collect sensitive personal information (race, political opinions, health data, biometrics, etc.).

3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process personal information based on the following legal grounds:

  • Consent (PIPA Art. 15(1)①, GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)) — Account registration, beta applications, marketing communications.
  • Performance of contract (GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)) — Providing the Service, processing transactions, customer support.
  • Legitimate interests (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)) — Service improvement, fraud prevention, security monitoring.
  • Legal obligation (PIPA Art. 15(1)②, GDPR Art. 6(1)(c)) — Tax records, regulatory compliance.

4. Purpose of Processing

  • To provide, maintain, and improve the Service
  • To authenticate your account and ensure security
  • To process credit transactions and marketplace operations
  • To communicate important service updates and security alerts
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • To prevent fraud, abuse, and Terms of Service violations

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy:

  • Account Data: Retained for the duration of your account. Deleted within 30 days of account deletion request, unless retention is required by law.
  • Transaction Records: Retained for 5 years from the date of transaction, as required by Korean tax and commercial law (상법 제33조, 국세기본법 제26조의2).
  • Usage Data (aggregated): Retained indefinitely in anonymized form.
  • Beta Application Data: Retained for the duration of the beta program plus 6 months.
  • Technical/Security Logs: Retained for up to 12 months.

After the retention period expires, personal information is promptly and irreversibly destroyed.

6. Your Rights

Under PIPA and GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right of Access — Request a copy of your personal information we hold.
  • Right to Correction — Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to Deletion — Request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Right to Suspend Processing — Request that we stop processing your personal information (PIPA Art. 37).
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — Withdraw your consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Right to Data Portability — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format (GDPR Art. 20).
  • Right to Object — Object to processing based on legitimate interests.

To exercise any of these rights, contact privacy@ogenti.com. We will respond within 10 days (PIPA) or 30 days (GDPR) of your request. We will not discriminate against you for exercising your rights.

7. International Data Transfers

Your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the Republic of Korea:

  • LLM Providers — When agents interact with AI providers (OpenAI, Inc.; Anthropic, PBC), the minimum required context is transmitted to their servers in the United States. Only the immediate conversation context is sent; no account data or personal identifiers are included.
  • Payment Processing — Stripe, Inc. (United States) processes payment card information. Stripe is PCI DSS Level 1 certified.
  • Hosting Infrastructure — Our servers are hosted by Vercel, Inc. (United States) and may replicate to global edge locations.

These transfers are conducted in compliance with PIPA Art. 17 (provision to third parties) and Art. 39-12 (overseas transfer), and where applicable, under EU Standard Contractual Clauses. We ensure that recipient entities maintain data protection standards equivalent to those required by applicable law.

8. Third-Party Services

  • Google Fonts — We load web fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com). Google may collect your IP address and browser information when fonts are loaded. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • LLM Providers — OpenAI and Anthropic APIs are used for agent intelligence. We send only the minimum context required. We do not store or log prompts on our servers. Refer to each provider's data usage policies.
  • Stripe — Payment processing. We do not store your full card number. Stripe's data practices are governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy.

9. Cookies & Tracking

The ogenti.com website does not use first-party cookies or tracking pixels. We do not use analytics services (no Google Analytics, no Facebook Pixel, etc.).

Third-party services loaded on this site (e.g., Google Fonts) may set their own cookies. You can control cookie behavior through your browser settings.

10. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children under 14 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 14. If you are between 14 and 18, you must have parental or guardian consent to use the Service, as stated in our Terms of Service. If we learn we have collected personal information from a child under 14 without verified parental consent, we will delete that information promptly. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 14, please contact privacy@ogenti.com.

11. Automated Decision-Making

The Service does not use fully automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects on users. Agent operations are tool-assisted processes that users configure and oversee. The credit exchange rate algorithm is a platform operational mechanism and does not constitute automated individual decision-making under GDPR Art. 22.

12. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information, including:

  • TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • Encryption at rest for stored personal data
  • Access controls restricting data access to authorized personnel
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments

Since agent data is stored locally on your device, you are responsible for implementing appropriate local security measures (device encryption, strong passwords, etc.).

13. Destruction of Personal Information

When personal information is no longer needed or the retention period has expired, we destroy it without delay using the following methods:

  • Electronic files: Permanently deleted using secure erasure methods that prevent recovery.
  • Physical records: Shredded or incinerated.

14. Complaints & Remedies

If you believe your personal information rights have been violated, you may:

  • Contact us at privacy@ogenti.com
  • File a complaint with the Personal Information Protection Commission (PIPC) of Korea: www.pipc.go.kr (☎ 1833-6972)
  • File a complaint with the Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA): privacy.kisa.or.kr (☎ 118)
  • EU residents may contact their local Data Protection Authority

15. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through a notice on the ogenti.com website or via email at least 7 days before taking effect. Continued use of the Service after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
